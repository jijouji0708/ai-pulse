## 1. OpenAI Shuts Down the Sora App and API

On March 24, OpenAI announced it will discontinue the Sora AI video generation service, including its iOS app, API, and website.

Just months after its public launch late last year, Sora peaked at 3.3 million downloads but had dropped to 1.1 million by February. The primary reason for the shutdown is the **enormous inference cost**. Revenue couldn't keep pace, so OpenAI is redirecting resources toward world-simulation research for robotics.

Sora 2 video generation will remain available within ChatGPT's paid plans.

**Key takeaway**: AI video generation is technically feasible, but building a sustainable business around it turned out to be far harder than expected.

## 2. Apple's New Siri Delayed Again — Pushed to iOS 26.5 Beta

The overhauled Siri, which was set to be rebuilt entirely on LLMs, has been delayed once more.

Originally slated for iOS 26.4, Bloomberg reports that issues with query processing accuracy and response speed have pushed it back to an iOS 26.5 beta release. Additionally, reports indicate that iOS 27 will open Siri up to third-party AI assistants beyond ChatGPT.

**Key takeaway**: Even a company the size of Apple struggles to bring LLMs to production quality. Their commitment to polish is admirable, but managing user expectations remains a challenge.

## 3. MCP (Model Context Protocol) Surpasses 97 Million Installs

Anthropic's Model Context Protocol (MCP) crossed 97 million installs in March.

MCP is a standard protocol that enables AI models to connect with external tools and data sources. Every major AI provider now ships MCP-compatible tools, marking its evolution from an experimental spec to core agent infrastructure.

**Key takeaway**: As AI agents move toward real-world deployment, having a common language for tool connectivity is a big deal. For developers, MCP support is quickly becoming a de facto requirement.
